# Strategy Frameworks

## Brief Diagnosis

Clarify the problem type before developing strategy:

- Awareness: the audience does not know the brand, product, or new offer.
- Understanding: the audience knows it but misunderstands its value.
- Preference: competitors feel more attractive or safer.
- Trust: claims need proof, authority, social evidence, or experience.
- Action: people need a reason to act now.
- Brand asset: the campaign must strengthen long-term recognition and meaning.

## Insight Structure

Use this pattern for useful insights:

- Audience truth: what people currently think, feel, or do.
- Category tension: what the category typically promises or ignores.
- Brand opportunity: what the brand can credibly own.
- Cultural hook: what larger context makes the idea timely.
- Strategic leap: what the campaign should make people believe or do.

## Strategy Output

Recommended structure:

1. Business context
2. Communication challenge
3. Audience definition
4. Key tension or insight
5. Brand role
6. Core proposition
7. Reasons to believe
8. Campaign task
9. Channel roles
10. Creative mandatories

## Strategy Director Deep Work Sequence

Use this sequence before writing the final strategy:

1. Fake demand vs. true pain
   - What did the client ask for?
   - What business pain may be hiding behind it?
   - What would happen if the brand solved the requested task but not the real pain?

2. Positioning
   - What market position is available?
   - What can the brand credibly own?
   - What competitor territory must be avoided?
   - Is the category dividend still growing, flattening, or disappearing?

3. Logic and evidence
   - What does the data prove?
   - What does the data fail to explain?
   - Which assumptions need validation?

4. Human insight
   - What fear, desire, greed, shame, aspiration, identity anxiety, or social emotion is underneath the behavior?
   - What does the audience say rationally?
   - What may they actually feel privately?

5. Creative bridge
   - What is the one core appeal that can excite the Creative Director?
   - What must creative work never lose?
   - What business logic can defend a bold creative idea?

## AI and Data Leverage

Use AI and data tools to accelerate, not replace, strategic judgment:

- Clean and cluster raw materials.
- Summarize industry and competitor patterns.
- Generate preliminary consumer personas.
- Compare claim territories and language patterns.
- Visualize strategy concepts for internal alignment.
- Pressure-test different positioning statements.

Always label AI-generated outputs as drafts or hypotheses until verified by human judgment, source materials, or additional research.

## Campaign Architecture

For integrated marketing, map stages:

- Warm-up: create attention, social context, teaser, KOL seed.
- Launch: deliver main idea, KV, hero video, main social topic.
- Amplify: UGC, PR, influencers, commerce, offline events.
- Convert: offer, landing page, CRM, livestream, retail activation.
- Sustain: content series, community, brand asset recap.
